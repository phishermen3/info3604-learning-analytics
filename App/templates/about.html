About This Research Project

xAPI Learning Analytics

What Are We Researching?

This project explores the student learning process using Experience API (xAPI). xAPI allows researchers to capture meaningful learning activities such as attempts, analyses, reflections, and problem-solving behaviors across digital learning environments. Our goal is to better understand learning patterns, engagement, and how students approach complex academic tasks.

Why Does This Matter?

Traditional grading systems only measure final outcomes. This research focuses on observing the learning process itself, how students learn, not just what score they receive. Insights from this study can help improve:

  • - Learning analytics and feedback systems
  • - Course design and teaching strategies
  • - Student support and personalized learning tools
  • - Future educational technologies

How Do You Contribute?

As a participant, you will simply record your learning actions on our logging system, LogStack, while working on your project - start to finish. Logs will be recorded as anonymous xAPI learning statements.

Your participation does not require extra work. We are observing learning interactions, not evaluating you personally. Every interaction you make helps researchers understand how students think, experiment, and improve over time. Your participation contributes to building smarter learning systems that can benefit future students.

Data collected is used strictly for academic research purposes and will be analyzed in aggregated and anonymized form. Student privacy is important to us. All collected data follows ethical research guidelines. Personal identities are not shared, and results are reported only at a group level.

xAPI Statement Components

xAPI statements consist of the components collected from your logs, which include: subject, verb, object, steps and stages. The subject refers to the learner performing an action, the verb describes the action being performed, and the object identifies what the action was performed on. Steps show the position of the learner in the structured problem-solving process of the course, while stages classify how the learner is engaging cognitively. Together, these components allow us to monitor learning behavior and progression in a detailed and flexible manner.
